# References
## Study Materials Used in the NotebookLM Knowledge Base

This document lists the main sources used to build the NotebookLM knowledge environment for this project.

The materials include books, scientific articles, educational resources, and multimedia content related to:

- strength training
- running physiology
- hybrid training
- sports nutrition
- exercise science

These sources were used to support the AI-assisted knowledge extraction process.

---

# 1. Books and Technical E-books

Thuany, M., & Gomes, T. N. (2023).  
**Road Running: Philosophy, Training, Performance, and Nutrition.**  
Editora UFS.

Evans, N. (2007).  
**Strength Training Anatomy.**  
A detailed guide explaining the biomechanics and muscle groups involved in resistance training exercises.

Charro, M. A., et al. (2018).  
**Strength Training: Training Structure and Load Control.**  
CREF4/SP Publication.

Tiagonutri.  
**Strength Training Guide: Methodologies and Applications.**  
E-book focusing on training progression principles and workout structure.

Coutinho, J.  
**Strength Training E-book.**  
Compilation of technical and practical articles from the website TreinamentoEsportivo.com.

Machado, A. (2011).  
**Running: Theory and Practice of Training.**  
A book focused on the physiological foundations of endurance training.

**Strength Training Manual.**  
Technical document covering resistance training principles and biomechanics.

Simões, A. P.  
**How to Start Running.**  
E-book providing medical and biomechanical guidance for beginner runners.

# 2. Scientific Articles and Academic Research

Thomas, D. T., Erdman, K. A., & Burke, L. M. (2016).  
**Nutrition and Athletic Performance.**  
Joint position statement by the Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics, Dietitians of Canada, and the American College of Sports Medicine.

Bertassoli, J. P. V. (2019).  
**Concurrent Training: Influence of Training Order on the Interference Effect.**  
Undergraduate thesis, University of Campinas (UNICAMP).

**Effects of Concurrent Training on Maximal and Explosive Strength in Trained Individuals.**  
Published in *Journal of Sports Sciences* (2025).

# Notes on Source Usage

The sources listed above were used to build the knowledge base uploaded to **NotebookLM**.  
The AI system analyzed these materials to generate insights, summaries, and structured study guides.

The objective of this project was not to replicate the original sources, but to demonstrate how **AI can assist in synthesizing knowledge across multiple domains of exercise science**.
